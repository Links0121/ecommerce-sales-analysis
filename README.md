# E-commerce Sales Data Analysis

## Project Overview
This project analyzes an e-commerce dataset containing over 113,000 orders to understand sales performance, customer behavior, and logistics efficiency.

The analysis focuses on identifying key business insights such as top-performing product categories, regional sales distribution, and the impact of discounts on profit.

## Dataset
The dataset contains 113,270 order records with 21 variables including:

- Customer information
- Product category
- Order details
- Shipping metrics
- Sales and profit

Key variables include:

- sales_per_order
- profit_per_order
- order_item_discount
- order_quantity
- order_date
- customer_region

## Analysis Performed

### 1. Monthly Sales Trend
Analyzed monthly sales to identify overall business growth patterns.

### 2. Sales by Product Category
Compared sales across product categories to identify the most profitable segments.

### 3. Regional Sales Distribution
Evaluated which regions generate the highest revenue.

### 4. Top Customers
Identified the top 10 customers contributing the most sales.

### 5. Discount vs Profit
Explored how discounts affect profitability.

### 6. Shipping Delay Analysis
Measured logistics performance by comparing scheduled vs actual shipping time.

## Tools Used

- Python
- Pandas
- Matplotlib
- Jupyter Notebook / Google Colab

## Key Insights

- Office Supplies generates the highest total sales.
- Sales vary significantly across regions.
- A small number of customers contribute a large portion of revenue.
- Higher discounts tend to reduce profit margins.
- Shipping delays occur in a measurable portion of orders.
